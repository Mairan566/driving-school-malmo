What is the Swedish theory test?

The Swedish driving theory test (kunskapsprov) is a mandatory exam you must pass before the practical driving test. It consists of 70 multiple-choice questions, of which 65 are scored (5 are unscored test questions). You need at least 52 correct answers out of 65 to pass (80%). You have 50 minutes to complete the test. The good news is that it's available in multiple languages including English and Arabic.

We offer a digital theory code (850 SEK) that gives you access to a complete online educational platform. It provides all the material you need to pass the exam at the test centre – traffic rules, road signs and risk awareness, all available in your own language. The platform also includes endless practice questions that mirror the real exam, so you can train until you feel confident. The Trafikverket exam fee is approximately 420 SEK (daytime) or 520 SEK (evening/weekend), paid directly to the test centre when you book.

A passed theory test is valid for 4 months. If you don't pass the practical driving test within that window, you'll need to retake the theory — so plan your timeline carefully and book your practical test shortly after passing the theory.

What does the test cover?

The Swedish theory test at the test centre includes questions on these key areas:

Traffic rules and road signs

Swedish traffic regulations, road signs, right-of-way rules and speed limits. You need to know what each sign means and how to act accordingly.

Risk awareness

Hazard perception, effects of alcohol, drugs and fatigue, and how to handle dangerous situations. This part is very important on the test.

Emergency situations

What to do at an accident, basic first aid, breakdown procedures on the road.

Vehicle safety

Vehicle maintenance, tyre requirements, seatbelts, child safety seats and eco-friendly driving.

Common mistakes to avoid on the theory test

Most failed attempts come down to a handful of recurring mistakes. Avoid these and your chances of passing on the first try go up dramatically:

  • Rushing through questions: You have 50 minutes for 70 questions — that's about 43 seconds each. Many students finish too quickly and make careless errors. Use the full time.
  • Confusing Swedish road signs: Some signs look similar to those in other countries but mean something different. The blue circular signs (påbudsmärken) are mandatory — they tell you what you MUST do, not what you CAN do.
  • Right-hand priority (högerregeln): Many international drivers don't have this rule in their home country. At unmarked intersections, traffic from the right ALWAYS has priority. This is tested heavily.
  • Underestimating the risk-awareness section: Questions about alcohol, drugs, and fatigue carry significant weight. Know the legal blood-alcohol limit (0.2‰) and the effects of fatigue on reaction time.
  • Not doing enough practice tests: The single best predictor of passing is how many practice tests you've completed. Aim for consistently scoring above 85% before booking the real test.
  • Ignoring eco-driving questions: Many students skip studying eco-driving because it seems obvious. But questions about fuel-efficient driving techniques, engine braking, and tyre pressure appear on every test.

Theory test in multiple languages

The Swedish theory test (kunskapsprov) can be taken in the following languages: Swedish, English, Arabic, Albanian, Bosnian, Croatian, Finnish, French, Persian, Russian, Sorani, Spanish, Turkish and German. You choose the language when you book your test at the test centre — the questions are identical regardless of language.
