What is the Swedish theory test?

The Swedish driving theory test (kunskapsprov) is a mandatory exam you must pass before the practical driving test. It consists of 70 multiple-choice questions, of which 65 are scored (5 are unscored test questions). You need at least 52 correct answers out of 65 to pass (80%). You have 50 minutes to complete the test. The good news is that it's available in multiple languages including English and Arabic!

What does the test cover?

The Swedish theory test at Trafikverket includes questions on these key areas:

Traffic rules and road signs

Swedish traffic regulations, road signs, right-of-way rules and speed limits. You need to know what each sign means and how to act accordingly.

Risk awareness

Hazard perception, effects of alcohol, drugs and fatigue, and how to handle dangerous situations. This part is very important on the test.

Emergency situations

What to do at an accident, basic first aid, breakdown procedures on the road.

Vehicle safety

Vehicle maintenance, tyre requirements, seatbelts, child safety seats and eco-friendly driving.

Theory test in multiple languages

The Swedish theory test (kunskapsprov) can be taken in the following languages: Swedish, English, Arabic, Albanian, Bosnian, Croatian, Finnish, French, Persian, Russian, Sorani, Spanish, Turkish and German. You choose the language when you book your test at Trafikverket — the questions are identical regardless of language.

Important information

The theory test is the first step on your journey to a driving licence. You must pass the theory test before you can book the practical driving test at Trafikverket. The test is taken on a computer at the Trafikverket centre at Cederströmsgatan 1, 212 39 Malmö.

The test duration is 50 minutes and consists of 70 multiple-choice questions, of which 65 are scored and 5 are unscored test questions. You can choose English when booking your test appointment, making it easier to understand the questions and answer with confidence. There is no limit on the number of attempts, but you must pay the Trafikverket fee each time.

The Trafikverket test fee is approximately 420 SEK (daytime) or 520 SEK (evening/weekend). This fee is separate from the theory code and is paid directly to Trafikverket when you book.

A passed theory test is valid for 4 months. If you do not pass the practical driving test within that period, you must retake the theory test. Plan your timeline carefully — we recommend booking your practical test shortly after passing the theory.
