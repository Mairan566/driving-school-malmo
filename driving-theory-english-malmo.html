The Swedish Theory Test Explained in English

The Swedish driving theory test (kunskapsprov) is a mandatory exam you must pass before your practical driving test. It consists of 70 multiple-choice questions and you need to answer at least 52 correctly to pass. The test is conducted at Trafikverket test centers and is available in multiple languages, including English.

At Andres Trafikskola, we offer two theory study options in English: a digital theory code that gives you access to an online study platform, or teacher-led theory lessons where Andre teaches you the traffic rules directly in English. Both options will fully prepare you for the Swedish theory test.

Topics Covered in the Swedish Theory Test

Traffic Rules

Right of way, speed limits, traffic signals, lane discipline, and overtaking rules in Sweden.

Road Signs

Warning signs, prohibitory signs, mandatory signs, and information signs used on Swedish roads.

Emergency Situations

What to do at an accident, first aid basics, breakdown procedures, and emergency services.

Eco-Friendly Driving

Fuel-efficient driving techniques, environmental impact of driving, and sustainable choices.

Risk Awareness

Understanding risks in traffic, alcohol and drugs, fatigue, and distracted driving.

Vehicle Safety

Vehicle maintenance, tire requirements, seatbelts, child safety seats, and cargo securing.

FAQ – Theory Test in English

Yes! The Swedish theory test (kunskapsprov) is available in many languages including English, Arabic, Spanish, Finnish, and more at Trafikverket test centers. You can choose your preferred language when booking the test.

The test covers: traffic rules and right of way, road signs, emergency situations, eco-friendly driving, vehicle safety and maintenance, risk awareness (alcohol, drugs, fatigue), and traffic environments (city, motorway, rural roads).

The Swedish theory test consists of 70 multiple-choice questions. You need to answer at least 52 correctly (about 74%) to pass. The test typically takes 50 minutes and is done on a computer.

There is no limit on how many times you can retake the Swedish theory test. You pay the test fee each time and can rebook as soon as you're ready. Use each attempt as a learning experience to identify gaps in your knowledge.
