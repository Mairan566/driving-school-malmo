Want to get a Swedish motorcycle licence? At Andre's Trafikskola, we offer motorcycle driving lessons with English-speaking instructors. MC licence categories in Sweden

Sweden has three motorcycle licence categories. Each has specific age requirements and power restrictions:

A1 – Light motorcycle

From age 16. Motorcycles up to 125 cc and 11 kW (15 hp). Ideal for younger riders or beginners. You can upgrade to A2 after turning 18.

A2 – Medium motorcycle

From age 18. Motorcycles up to 35 kW (47 hp). The most popular starting category for adult riders. After 2 years with A2, you can upgrade to the full A licence from age 20.

A – Full motorcycle licence

From age 24 (or from age 20 if you have held A2 for at least 2 years). No power restrictions – ride any motorcycle.

Which one is right for you?

Not sure which category suits your situation? Contact us and we will discuss your experience, age and goals to recommend the best path to your licence.

What the training includes

The Swedish motorcycle training covers several key areas:

Manoeuvre training

Start with fundamental skills on a practice area: slow-speed balance, figure eights, slalom, emergency braking and swerving. These exercises build the muscle memory you need for safe riding.

City riding

Ride in real Malmö traffic: intersections, roundabouts, lane changes, pedestrians and cyclists. Learn to read traffic flow and position yourself safely on the road.

Rural and highway riding

Practice on country roads and highways outside Malmö. Learn cornering technique, overtaking, speed adaptation and how to handle varying road conditions and wind.

Risk training

Two mandatory risk courses: Riskettan (theory on alcohol, drugs and fatigue, 750 SEK) and Risktvåan MC (practical skid pad training, 3,200 SEK). Both are required before your driving test.

Steps to your MC licence

1

Apply for learner's permit

Start by applying for a learner's permit (körkortstillstånd) from Transportstyrelsen. You need an eye test and a health declaration. The application is straightforward and done online.

2

Book your first lesson

Call us to book your first lesson. We assess your experience level, help you choose the right category (A1, A2 or A) and create a training plan that suits you.

3

Practical riding training

Train with our experienced instructors on the practice area and in traffic. Training includes manoeuvres (at low and high speed) and riding in different environments. We adapt to your level and goals.

4

Theory study

Alongside your riding lessons, study theory using books, apps or our digital materials. Learn traffic rules, risk awareness and motorcycle-specific topics like balance, road positioning and visibility.

5

Complete risk training (Riskettan & Risktvåan MC)

MC risk training has two parts: Risk 1 MC – theory on hazards, alcohol, drugs and fatigue. Risk 2 MC – practical exercises focusing on riding style and safety. Both must be completed before the tests and are valid for 5 years.

6

Book and pass theory test

Book the theory test at Trafikverket when you feel ready. The test has 65 questions and you need at least 52 correct to pass. You can prepare with our digital materials. The theory test must be passed before the riding test.

7

Driving test and get your licence

When you are ready, we book the riding test at Trafikverket. The test consists of a manoeuvre part (low and high speed) and a traffic riding part where your safety and control are assessed. After passing, you receive the result immediately and your licence arrives by post.
