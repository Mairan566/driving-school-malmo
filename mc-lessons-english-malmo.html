MC licence categories in Sweden

Sweden has three motorcycle licence categories. Each has specific age requirements and power restrictions:

A1 – Light motorcycle

From age 16. Motorcycles up to 125 cc and 11 kW (15 hp). Ideal for younger riders or those who want to start with a smaller bike. You can upgrade to A2 after turning 18.

A2 – Medium motorcycle

From age 18. Motorcycles up to 35 kW (47 hp). The most popular starting category for adult riders. After 2 years with A2, you can upgrade to full A licence from age 20.

A – Full motorcycle licence

From age 24 (or from age 20 if you have held A2 for at least 2 years). No power restrictions – ride any motorcycle. Requires a separate driving test even when upgrading from A2.

Which one is right for you?

Not sure which category suits your situation? Contact us and we will discuss your experience, age and goals to recommend the best path to your licence.

What the MC curriculum includes

The Swedish motorcycle education covers several key areas. Here is what you can expect during your training:

Manoeuvring exercises

Start with fundamental skills on a practice area: slow-speed balance, figure eights, slalom, emergency braking and swerving. These exercises build the muscle memory you need for safe riding.

City traffic riding

Ride in real Malmö traffic: intersections, roundabouts, lane changes, pedestrians and cyclists. Learn to read traffic flow and position yourself safely.

Rural and highway riding

Practice on country roads and highways outside Malmö. Learn cornering technique, overtaking, speed adaptation and how to handle varying road conditions and wind.

Risk education (Riskettan & Risktvåan)

Two mandatory risk courses: Riskettan (theory on alcohol, drugs, fatigue, 750 SEK) and Risktvåan MC (practical skid pad training, 3,200 SEK). Both are required before your driving test.

Theory preparation

Study for the Trafikverket theory test using digital material. The test has 65 questions and you need at least 52 correct to pass. We explain everything in English.

3

Practical riding lessons

Start with manoeuvring exercises, then progress to city traffic, residential areas, country roads and highway. Each lesson is 80 minutes.

4

Driving test at Trafikverket

The final test includes a manoeuvring part and a traffic part. You can rent our motorcycle for the test (1,200 SEK). After passing, you receive your Swedish MC licence.

What to expect as an international student

Moving to Sweden and getting a motorcycle licence involves some specific considerations. Here is what you should know:

If you already hold a motorcycle licence from an EU/EEA country, you can generally continue riding in Sweden. Non-EU licences are typically valid for one year after you register as a Swedish resident, after which you need a Swedish licence. In most cases, this means going through the full Swedish licensing process including theory test, risk courses and driving test.

All official communication with Trafikverket (the Swedish Transport Administration) is in Swedish, but the theory test itself is available in English. Our role is to bridge the language gap – we explain all concepts, rules and procedures in English so you can focus on learning to ride safely.
