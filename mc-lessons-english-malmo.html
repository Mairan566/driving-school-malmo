Motorcycle Driving School in Malmö – English Instruction Available

Moving to Sweden and want to get a motorcycle license? Already have a foreign motorcycle license but need a Swedish one? Andres Trafikskola in Malmö offers motorcycle driving lessons with English-speaking instructors to help internationals navigate the Swedish licensing system.

Getting a motorcycle license in Sweden follows a structured process that includes a theory test, two mandatory risk education courses (Riskettan and Risktvåan), practical riding lessons, and a final driving test at Trafikverket. We guide you through each step and make sure you understand the requirements – all explained in English.

We offer all three motorcycle license categories: A1 (from age 16, up to 125cc/11kW), A2 (from age 18, up to 35kW), and A (from age 24, unrestricted). Our modern motorcycles – Yamaha MT-07 35KW and BMW F800 GS – are well-maintained and perfect for learning.

How to Get a Swedish Motorcycle License

  1. Theory Test at Trafikverket

    Pass the motorcycle theory test. The test is available in Swedish, but you can prepare using English-language study materials. We advise on the best resources.

  2. Riskettan (Risk 1 – Theory)

    Mandatory risk education covering alcohol, drugs, fatigue, and motorcycle-specific traffic risks. Takes approximately 3 hours. Cost: 750 SEK.

  3. Risktvåan for MC (Risk 2 – Practical)

    Practical skid pad training covering emergency braking, evasive maneuvers and riding on slippery surfaces. Note: This is different from car Risk 2. Cost: 3,200 SEK.

  4. Riding Lessons

    Start with track exercises to build fundamental skills, then progress to real traffic riding in Malmö. Each lesson is 80 minutes of focused riding.

  5. Driving Test at Trafikverket

    The final test includes track exercises and traffic riding. After passing, you receive your Swedish motorcycle license. Congratulations!

Already Have a Motorcycle License from Another Country?

If you have a motorcycle license from an EU/EEA country, you may be able to exchange it for a Swedish license without additional tests. For non-EU licenses, you typically need to complete the Swedish licensing process. Contact us to discuss your specific situation – we can advise you on the most efficient path to a Swedish MC license.
