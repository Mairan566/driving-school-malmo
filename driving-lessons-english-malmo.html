Learn to drive in Malmö — in your own language

Welcome to our driving school in Malmö! We offer specialised lessons for international residents and expats in Malmö and the surrounding areas. Our instructor André is fluent in English and explains Swedish traffic rules in clear, everyday language. Our school is located at Ystadvägen 22B, 214 30 Malmö.

Learning to drive without language barriers reduces stress and speeds up comprehension. When you understand rules and instructions clearly, your driving becomes safer and more confident. We offer comprehensive training covering all stages of getting your licence: from theory preparation and risk courses to practical lessons and the final driving test at Trafikverket on Cederströmsgatan 1 in Malmö.

Steps to your Swedish driving licence

1

Apply for learner's permit

Start by applying for a learner's permit (körkortstillstånd) from Transportstyrelsen. You need an eye test and a health declaration. The application is straightforward and done online.

2

Book your first lesson

Call us to book your first lesson. We assess your experience level and create a training plan that suits your goals and schedule.

3

Practical driving training

Train with our instructor Andre on the streets of Malmö. Each lesson is 60 minutes of focused training on intersections, roundabouts, parking, city driving and highway driving.

4

Theory study

Alongside your driving lessons, study theory using the digital theory code, which gives you access to a complete educational platform. Learn traffic rules, road signs and risk awareness.

5

Complete risk training (Riskettan & Risktvåan)

Risk training consists of two parts: Riskettan (theory on alcohol, drugs and fatigue) and Risktvåan (practical skid pad training). Both parts must be completed before the driving test.

6

Driving test and get your licence

When you are ready, we book the driving test at Trafikverket. You must pass the theory test first, then the practical driving test. After passing, your licence arrives by post!

What does the driving test involve?

The practical driving test (uppkörning) at Trafikverket takes approximately 45 minutes. It starts with a vehicle safety check where the examiner asks you to demonstrate checks on the car (lights, tyres, fluid levels). Then you drive in both city traffic and on country/highway roads. The examiner evaluates your ability to drive safely and independently.

The test is conducted at Trafikverket's test centre at Cederströmsgatan 1, Malmö. We practise on the actual routes used during the test so you feel confident on test day. The driving test fee is approximately 1,000 SEK (daytime) or 1,400 SEK (evening/weekend), paid directly to Trafikverket.

Key Swedish traffic rules for English speakers

If you're used to driving in another country, some Swedish rules may surprise you. Here are the most important differences:

  • Right-hand traffic: Sweden drives on the right. If you're from the UK, Ireland, or another left-hand-drive country, this is the biggest adjustment.
  • Högerregeln (right-hand rule): At unmarked intersections, traffic from the right has priority. This applies in most residential areas and catches many international drivers off guard.
  • Headlights always on: Swedish law requires dipped headlights (halvljus) at all times, day and night, all year round.
  • Speed limits: 30 km/h in school zones, 50 km/h in urban areas, 70–90 km/h on country roads, 110–120 km/h on motorways. Speed cameras are common.
  • Zero tolerance for mobile phones: Using a handheld phone while driving is illegal and strictly enforced.
  • Winter tyres required: From 1 December to 31 March, winter tyres are mandatory when conditions require them.

During your lessons, we cover all of these rules and more — explained clearly so you understand not just WHAT the rule is, but WHY it exists.
